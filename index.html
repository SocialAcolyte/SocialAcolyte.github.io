Molecular Curation // Silica Fiber Matrix

Visualizing the thermal insulation of a Space Shuttle silica tile (LI-900). Your cursor acts as a localized plasma heat vector. As you glide across the boundary layer (top), it injects high-density kinetic thermal particles. These particles flow downwards, colliding, deflecting, and transferring speed to the microscopic glass fiber lattice, which vibrates in response. The heat stays localized at the re-entry boundary, dissipating into the dark, while the structural skin (bottom) remains cold and silent.
