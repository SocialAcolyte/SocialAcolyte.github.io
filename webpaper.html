What it does

Three things, on every page.

01 Rewrites

Type a request — “dark editorial layout”, “just the recipe” — and the live page becomes it. Or let it auto-tune type, spacing, and colour on its own.

02 Cleans

Ads, cookie walls, engagement-bait, comment sludge — stripped before you ever read a word. A calm page instead of a battlefield.

03 Knows you

Write who you are once. webpaper carries that context everywhere and weights each page toward what actually matters to you.

A model that can rewrite any page, in any tone, tuned to you — is a propaganda engine. The only thing that beats it is the same power, in your hands.

The machinery that deletes an ad can inject a lie. The machinery that strips bias can manufacture it — aimed at you specifically. Someone will build this. The only open question is whose hands hold it.

So webpaper is open-source and strictly non-commercial. No telemetry, no account on our end, no profit motive to quietly turn it against you. The page, your prompt, and your own AI session are the entire loop. This is infrastructure for memetic self-defense — for the people, by the people.

Install · beta 0.0.1

Running in about a minute.

Download webpaper.zip

Requires a Chromium browser (Chrome, Edge, Brave, Arc) and a Claude account — the free tier works. webpaper borrows your existing session and never stores your credentials.

  1. 1Unzip the download. Keep the folder somewhere permanent — Chrome loads from it directly.
  2. 2Open chrome://extensions and turn on Developer mode (top-right).
  3. 3Click Load unpacked and select the unzipped webpaper folder.
  4. 4Pin webpaper, click it, and connect the AI you already use when prompted.
  5. 5Flip it on. Go anywhere. Try a prompt — or a mode.
